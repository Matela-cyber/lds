Digital Innovators Summit 2025

Building Digital Futures: Artificial Intelligence, Integrity and Inclusion

Speakers

Mathe Victoria Ntšekhe (Mx)

Lecturer - NUL Faculty of Mathematics and Computer Science | Intersectional African Feminist

Mathe Ntšekhe is an intersectional African feminist and a lecturer at the National University of Lesotho in the department of Mathematics and Computer Science.

Mathe's research interest lies in the area of Information and Communication Technologies for Development, also known as "ICT4D". This field in essence deals with the questions of how ICTs (i.e. digital technologies) may be used "for good" to advance human development.

Mathe will be highly valuable to the Digital Innovators Summit due to their strong academic background in digital technologies and their applications, as well as ensuring that the summit's discussions consider diverse and marginalized communities, promoting inclusivity in the African context.

Topics: ICT4D, Digital Inclusion, African Feminism in Tech

Lineo Segoete (Ms)

Writer | Artistic Research-Practitioner | Cultural Producer

Lineo Segoete is a versatile professional writer, artistic research-practitioner and cultural producer.

With creative roots set in storytelling in its various expressions, her work is grounded in critical literacies spanning across history, media and arts curriculum and pedagogy. Segoete's practice revolves around cultural production and the cultivation of creative heritage through collaborations with other academics and artists.

She has operated within the cultural space as co-director and co-founder of Ba Re E Ne Re Literally Arts, co-convenor of The Pan-African arts research collective Another Roadmap Africa Cluster (ARAC), a 2016-2017 Humphrey Fellow, 2024 Museumslab Fellow and as one half of ART.1.ST consultancy, which leads the Lesotho chapter of Chimére Communities.

Her background in media and arts, as well as her expertise in storytelling, critical literacies and cultural production are essential to the Digital Innovators Summit, as she will contribute insight into the cultural and artistic implications of misinformation and malinformation.

Topics: Storytelling, Cultural Production, Misinformation in Arts

Rethabile Tshephe (Ms)

Cyber Researcher | AI Ethics Researcher

Rethabile Tsephe is a Researcher with extensive knowledge in cyber related topics that include cyber psychology, cyber mental health and cyber disorders.

She has contributed in two international white papers, one being "Cyber Psychology and Wellbeing", which can be found on researchgate.com. In addition to the above mentioned. She is currently an AI Ethics Researcher for the Pan-African Center for AI Ethics in Nigeria, a not-for-profit organization shaping the future of ethical AI in Africa and bridging Innovation and integrity across Africa. In 2023, she was one of the panelists at the DCN Global Africa Launch in Zambia under the theme "Threats and Opportunities of New Technologies", that highlighted AI technology.

Rethabile's insights into the psychological impacts of AI, as well as her work with the Pan-African Centre for AI Ethics, will be crucial in addressing mental and ethical considerations in the use of AI in the context of combating misinformation and malinformation, making her an invaluable contributor to the Digital Innovation Summit.

Topics: Cyber Psychology, AI Ethics, Mental Health in Digital Spaces

Tšeliso Monaheng (Mr)

Writer | Photographer | Videographer | Documentarian

Tšeliso Monaheng is a multifaceted creative professional with expertise in writing, photography, videography, social media strategy, and audio engineering.

He has led major music projects in Africa, including the Lesotho Rap Archives in 2023 alongside Tello "Dallas T" Leballo, documenting the Southern African rap scene through text, audio, and visuals.

Holding a Degree in Computer Science from the University of Cape Town, Tšeliso's writing has been featured in The Guardian and The Fader, while his photography has appeared in Le Monde and the Grammy's online platform. He has contributed to international documentaries and co-directed films like "A Gentle Magic." Tšeliso has managed social media campaigns for Concerts SA and Music in Africa and recorded vocals for prominent musicians and podcasts.

Tšeliso's diverse background makes him an ideal contributor to the Digital Innovators Summit, where his insights will be crucial in discussing the pros and cons of AI. His experience in various media forms, combined with his technical expertise, will help analyze AI's impact on digital media, highlighting both its transformative potential and ethical challenges.

Topics: Digital Media, AI in Creative Industries, Multimedia Storytelling

Dr. Mugambi Laibuta

Advocate of the High Court of Kenya | Data Protection Specialist

Dr. Mugambi Laibuta is an esteemed Advocate of the High Court of Kenya and a Certified Information Privacy Manager.

With over 14 years of experience as a licensed legal practitioner, he is a trained mediator, legislative counsel, policy analyst, and a specialist in privacy and data protection.

Dr. Laibuta recently completed his PhD at the University of the Witwatersrand, where he focused on Privacy and Data Protection in Kenya. His extensive expertise includes policy development, governance, rule of law, constitutionalism, legislative drafting, digital rights, internet and technology law, privacy, and data protection. He also imparts his knowledge as a lecturer in Legal Writing and Legislative Drafting at the Kenya School of Law.

As the Chairperson of the Data Privacy and Governance Society of Kenya, Dr. Laibuta's leadership and insights into data privacy are invaluable. His participation in the Digital Innovators Summit will provide attendees with a profound understanding of the legal and ethical implications of AI in combating misinformation. His diverse expertise and practical experience in policy and governance will significantly enhance the discourse on digital rights and data protection, making a substantial impact on the summit's goals.

Topics: Data Privacy, AI Law, Digital Rights

Letsatsi Lekhooa

President - Internet Society Lesotho | Technology and Climate Change Researcher

Letsatsi Phinehas Lekhooa is a seasoned technology and climate change researcher with a robust academic background in mathematics and computer science.

His expertise is further enriched by specialized training in climate change adaptation, mitigation, and policy. Letsatsi is dedicated to advancing environmentally friendly technological solutions and effective climate policy development.

His extensive experience in stakeholder engagement, project coordination, and policy analysis allows him to excel in evaluating and enhancing climate change, environmental, and ICT policies. He is adept at developing strategic recommendations and fostering collaboration among diverse stakeholders.

As the President of the Internet Society Lesotho, Letsatsi Lekhooa is the perfect speaker for the Digital Innovators Summit. His unique blend of technological acumen and environmental advocacy positions him at the forefront of contemporary digital challenges. His insights into the intersection of ICT and climate policy are invaluable for attendees seeking to leverage technology for sustainable development.

Topics: Climate Tech, Digital Policy, Sustainable Innovation

Moderators

Nkopane Ntholi (Mr)

Radio Personality | Voiceover Artist | Social Media Influencer

Nkopane Ntholi is a distinguished voiceover artist and radio personality, known for his dynamic presence on Sky Alpha HD.

With a background in Public Administration and Political Science, Nkopane is also a passionate youth rights activist.

His diverse expertise and engaging communication skills make him an ideal moderator for the AI summit, where he will bring insightful perspectives and vibrant discussions to the forefront.

Topics: Media Engagement, Youth Advocacy

Refiloe "Uncle Fif" Thoahlane (Mr)

Content Creator | Performing Artist | Mental Health Activist

Refiloe Thoahlane is a multitalented creative professional, with experience in music, radio, content creation, and now teaching.

With a strong social media presence, he uses his platform to tackle important societal issues such as mental health awareness.

His experience as a radio personality, as well as his engaging presence, make him the ideal man to moderate the discussions that will be the focus of the Digital Innovators Summit.

Topics: Mental Health in Digital Spaces, Content Creation

Tsietsi Matjele (Mr)

Lecturer - Limkokwing University of Creative Technology

Mr. Tsietsi Matjele is a distinguished lecturer in the Faculty of Information Communication and Technology at Limkokwing University.

With over four years of experience in academic teaching. He holds a professional qualification in Computer Science, specializing in soft-computing. His expertise encompasses data mining, artificial intelligence, machine learning, web development, and cybersecurity.

Mr. Matjele's diverse experience spans data analysis, network and computer support, digital design, and video production. He has served as an adjudicator at the UNDP STEAM conference and as a panelist at the Sebabatso Youth Empowerment Initiative. Beyond his academic and professional roles, he is an entrepreneur and the founder of Newles Technologies, an IT consultancy that provides bespoke IT solutions.

With a solid background in computer science, Mr. Matjele has honed his skills in developing intelligent algorithms and building predictive models. He has a keen interest in the ethical implications of AI and is an advocate for responsible AI applications.

Mr. Matjele's comprehensive understanding of AI and its ethical dimensions, coupled with his practical experience in technology and entrepreneurship, make him exceptionally well-suited to speak at the Digital Innovators Summit. His insights into AI, machine learning, and ethical considerations in technology will provide invaluable perspectives to the summit's focus on enhancing digital media skills and combating misinformation.

Topics: AI Ethics, Machine Learning, Cybersecurity

Spotlight Speakers

Professor Mosotho George

Senior Academic - National University of Lesotho

Professor Mosotho George, a distinguished scholar and senior academic at the National University of Lesotho, brings a formidable blend of intellect, vision, and commitment to advancing digital innovation across Southern Africa.

With an academic career grounded in chemical sciences and emerging technologies—including computational techniques in studying complex analytical data—Professor George has played a leading role in building research ecosystems that respond to Africa's socio-economic challenges. His leadership has nurtured home-grown academic excellence and fostered regional collaborations aimed at leveraging artificial intelligence (AI) for sustainable development. Renowned for integrating research, policy, and community engagement, he serves as a vital link between academia and national innovation systems. His presence at the Digital Innovators Summit highlights the critical role of higher education in shaping Africa's future in the Fourth Industrial Revolution.

At #DIS2025, Professor George will speak on "AI Talent and the Role of Higher Education," a theme deeply aligned with his scholarly and institutional mission. He will share insights on how universities can drive digital fluency, build research capacity, and cultivate entrepreneurial thinking among young Africans. As the continent navigates rapid technological shifts and the demand for inclusive growth, his perspective will spark dialogue on curriculum reform, innovation ecosystems, and multi-sectoral collaboration. In an era where AI is redefining every aspect of life, Professor George's voice is both timely and transformative—championing a digital future that is intellectually grounded, locally relevant, and globally competitive.

Topics: AI in Higher Education, Research Ecosystems, Digital Innovation

Mr Kanono Ramashamole

Principal Secretary - Ministry of Information, Communications, Science, Technology and Innovation

Mr Kanono Ramashamole stands at the helm of Lesotho's digital transformation agenda with a resolute focus on inclusivity and modernisation.

Under his stewardship, MICSTI has launched the ambitious "50-in-5" campaign, aligning Lesotho with global efforts to establish Digital Public Infrastructure within five years. This includes the expansion of e-services, digital identity systems, and payment gateways, all designed to ensure equitable access to essential services and economic participation.

At the 2025 Digital Innovators Summit, Mr Ramashamole will delve into the theme "Policy Foundations for AI-Driven Development," offering insights into how Lesotho is crafting regulatory ecosystems and data infrastructure to responsibly harness artificial intelligence. His leadership exemplifies a commitment to ethical governance and technological advancement, positioning Lesotho as a proactive player in Africa's digital future. His participation promises to shed light on the strategic frameworks necessary for fostering innovation while upholding democratic values and inclusivity.

Topics: Digital Governance, AI Policy, Public Infrastructure

H.E. Harry MacDonald

British High Commissioner to the Kingdom of Lesotho

His Excellency Harry MacDonald brings a wealth of diplomatic experience to his role, having previously served in diverse postings including Indonesia, Austria, Afghanistan, and the Democratic Republic of Congo.

Recognising the transformative potential of artificial intelligence (AI), High Commissioner MacDonald has actively engaged in initiatives that promote digital literacy and capacity-building among Basotho youth. Through programmes like the Chevening Scholarship, he has encouraged graduates to acquire advanced skills in the United Kingdom and return to contribute meaningfully to Lesotho's socio-economic growth.

At the 2025 Digital Innovators Summit, High Commissioner MacDonald will address the theme "Ethical AI and Global Partnerships," reflecting his dedication to ensuring that technological advancements align with democratic values and human rights. He advocates for international cooperation that supports Lesotho's aspirations to harness AI responsibly, particularly in sectors like renewable energy, where the UK has pledged to assist in developing clean energy solutions. His emphasis on respect and mutual understanding underscores the UK's approach to diplomacy, aiming to empower local communities while fostering global partnerships. Through his leadership, the British High Commission in Maseru continues to play a pivotal role in supporting Lesotho's journey towards a digitally inclusive and ethically grounded future.

Topics: Ethical AI, Global Partnerships, Digital Diplomacy

H.E. Ntšiuoa Sekete

Lesotho's Ambassador to Ethiopia and Permanent Representative to the African Union

Her Excellency Ntšiuoa Sekete brings a distinguished record of diplomatic service and strategic policy leadership.

With over two decades of experience in continental governance and development communication—spanning roles at the Pan-African Parliament and the African Capacity Building Foundation—she has consistently championed ethical governance, gender equality, and institutional reform. Her recent efforts in securing Lesotho's ratification of key African Union instruments, including the Charter on Public Service and the Convention on Cybersecurity and Personal Data Protection, affirm her commitment to aligning national priorities with Africa's broader digital and governance frameworks.

In the context of this year's theme, "Building Digital Futures: Artificial Intelligence, Integrity and Inclusion," Ambassador Sekete's contribution offers a crucial lens into multilateral diplomacy's role in shaping Africa's digital transformation. Drawing on her expertise in development management and media practice, she advocates for policy coherence, youth inclusion, and ethical AI adoption across the continent. Her participation underscores the necessity of harmonised regional cooperation in ensuring that AI technologies advance inclusive growth, democratic values, and sustainable innovation—making her voice indispensable in the continent's digital discourse.

Topics: Digital Governance, African Union Policy, Ethical AI

Hon. Mohlomi Moleko

Lesotho's Minister of Natural Resources

Hon. Mohlomi Moleko brings a visionary approach to #DIS2025, grounded in sustainability, digital transformation, and inclusive growth.

His leadership in initiatives like the Lesotho Highlands Water Project, smart mining, and wetland conservation reflects a commitment to aligning natural resource management with innovation and accountability. By promoting technological adoption in sectors like mining and water, and advocating for youth employment and environmental stewardship, Moleko is shaping a progressive model for resource governance.

At the Digital Innovators Summit, his contribution under the theme "Building Digital Futures: Artificial Intelligence, Integrity and Inclusion" is especially significant. His focus on applying AI to optimize resource management, support artisanal miners, and tackle corruption aligns with the summit's goals of ethical and inclusive digital progress.

Topics: AI in Resource Management, Sustainable Tech, Digital Governance
